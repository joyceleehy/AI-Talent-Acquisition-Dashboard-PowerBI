# AI Talent Acquisition Dashboard — Key Insights

## Executive Summary
847 hires YTD with an 82.7% offer acceptance rate suggests healthy pipeline conversion overall — but Engineering, Sales, and Operations show signs of structural hiring strain that risk impacting business targets.

---

## 1. Overall Hiring Health

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total applications | 14,382 |
| Total hires YTD | 847 (+67 vs last year) |
| Offer acceptance rate | 82.7% |
| Avg time to fill | 33 days |
| Open requisitions | 213 |

Year-over-year improvement in hires and strong offer acceptance suggest the recruiting pipeline is functioning well at a macro level. The risk lies in specific departments and aging roles.

---

## 2. Department Risk

Engineering carries the highest hiring risk — most open requisitions, slowest movement, and days-to-fill above the peer threshold (4.2 vs 3.4 avg). Sales and Operations follow with elevated aging requisition counts.

Slow hiring in Engineering likely signals either recruiter capacity constraints, high bar for technical screening, or insufficient sourcing pipeline for specialist roles.

---

## 3. Aging Requisitions

Roles aged over 60 and 90 days are concentrated in Engineering, Sales, and Operations. The longer a role remains open, the higher the business cost — through delayed productivity, increased workload on existing team members, and compounding sourcing spend.

---

## 4. Source of Hire

LinkedIn and employee referrals are the strongest performing channels by hire volume. Lower-performing sources warrant a sourcing ROI review before the next hiring cycle budget is set.

---

## 5. Offer Acceptance

82.7% offer acceptance is strong but not exceptional. Offer declines — even at a small rate — in high-demand roles like Engineering should be investigated for compensation competitiveness or competing offer timelines.

---

## Final Summary

Hiring health is positive at the aggregate level but masks department-level risk. Engineering requires immediate attention across sourcing, screening capacity, and offer competitiveness. A weekly aging requisition review for roles over 60 days would address the most acute bottlenecks.
